My First Local DESIGN GiG

Churchplus

Churchplus is a church management application used by churches to manage members, communicate with and have members communicate back with them and finally, allow new or returning members easily locate the church, its branches and cells around the State.

Let’s Begin With The Problem We Were Tackling

In 2020, the world was battling the pandemic of covid-19 and suddenly, the organization I had just joined as a UI/UX Designer. My first main design role at any organization.

They decided to take advantage of people likely being indoors for a long time.

One thing about we Africans, most actually, is that we are very religious and would take faith over proof. So when churches were asked not to let their members into physical buildings. People murmured and frowned about it.

So Malon Technologies, decided to bring hope to these people, meaning you could connect with local churches without leaving your house.

This was a problem because only a very few churches (insignificant number compared to the population of churches in Nigeria) had fully embraced technology.

Our First Meeting/My Role In The Project

Yes, we had our first meeting overview with the whole team. At this point, the company was pivoting from a digital agency to a product owned company, so this project was going to be taken on priority.

Our meeting was to discuss the PRD already created for the project. The aim was for the product owner to explain it deeply, discuss roles and see if any suggestions or issues would be raised.

Eventually, it got to my role, at this point, I hadn’t worked on a live project ever. Just daily ui challenges from the past.

I was nervous, really sweaty, yet I was open to embracing the challenge.
So my role was to bring the product requirement document to life as a designer.

Things I was going to do were:
1. UX Research
2. IA/Low-fi/High-fi designs
3. Usability Testing

Note:  At this point I wasn’t sure what to research or how the project might look because it was the first time I was hearing about the project, so I had to take a while to learn about the PRD and be sure I understood the project well enough.

User Research

Research was broken into two forms:
Qualitative
Quantitative

I began the qualitative research to help understand how a few churches already solve their issues. Which of them had started deploying online solution to stay abreast with their members in the no-contact times and which hadn’t. Fortunately, we had team members who had connection with the admins of some churches, so calls were set up with at least 5 chruch admins. We had the calls more than once with about 2 of the admins.

Some of the questions asked asides the informal introductions were open ended like:

1. The times aren’t favourable for many of us because we have to be at home all the time, can you walk me through how your church works in these times?
2. Has there been a time in the past where physical meetings were impossible and the church had to improvise, what did you do in these times and what caused this eventuality?
3. In these times, are there things that have taken a negative turn in terms of the chruch structure, what are those and how does the church plan on mitigating the risk of decline?

and more questions, especially follow-up questions.

When the qualitative interviews were done, I picked the results and ran more quantitative interviews:
Surveys to see that these were common challenges and concerns acorss multiple churches.

The fifth meeting was scheduled to present the high-fidelity designs before it was handed off to developers.

I had shared my rationale across most of the pages, as they were so much.

Learnings

I learnt about screen sizes and design scalability. Testing designs across several screens and ensuring they all look good across several screens.

I learnt about involving developers early on and ensuring they give feedback much early.

I also learnt about documenting designs as I had to go back and forth explaining what each designs did as developers were developing it.

I also learnt about arranging the files better, using components etc.

Final Overview of The High-fidelity Designs

Challenges/Learnings

I was done with my designs and devs were developing it rapidly. At the end of development we discovered we had resolution issues, the dashboard looked different on larger screens and the links on the sidebar didn’t fit well even on smaller screens because I had to scroll to get to all the links.

So I needed to readjust the designs, at that time, I used very little components.

While adjusting, I felt certain designs would work better, so I had to rework the whole designs....
