My First Int’l DESIGN GiG

DPhi’s Landing Pages

DPhi’s landing pages had a need for redesigning to suit its vision.I must state that one of the reasons for hiring young interns at the time was to bring freshness into the Organization as well as help interpret its vision to a diverse user group.

Let’s Begin With The Problem We Were Tackling

When I joined DPhi, the exisiting designs didn’t fully interpret the vision of the organization especially the information hierarchy on the main landing page. Not to talk about the visual appeal as well. Also, the interaction design wasn’t great especially for the data challenge pages. It looked cluttered and still didn’t interpret the vision of DPhi and make the company look like a worthwhile competitor to its competition.

My Role On The Project

I was assigned a few landing pages to work on while some where assigned to other designers.
The ones I was assigned are the: DPhi’s main homepage, individual landing page, live-session landing page, assessment landing page,about us page, business landing page.
I was the sole UI designer for these pages while feedback was delivered by the UX team as well as the project manager, the engineering team, other ui designers and ocassionally, the product owner.

Individual Landing Page

Apparently, this was my first project for DPhi, I began by looking at the existing landing page. Analysing what worked and what didn’t in comparison with the vision that was supposed to be captured on the page. Then I structured the process into:

  • Researching on landing pages in the same space that were already successful like datacamp, dataquest, hackerrank, codecademy etc.

  • Then I created an information heirarchy which was the backbone of the homepage. This took quite a while, as I had to request for feedback severally.

  • When the heirarchy looked clear enough and capturing of the vision of DPhi, I proceeded to creating the low-fi designs Finally, I created the high-fidelity designs.

Overview of The Design Process

Conclusion

In between the designs, we had routine calls to discuss the status and feedback was always given, which I worked on and presented in a follow-up routine call. It was as intense as my next coming projects, maybe the most intense I had ever been in until then, as it was my first design for DPhi. In the end, I had learned about design consistency, UX laws, design laws like balance, rhythm, etc.
